## What I Learned

This room focuses on the **Blue Team** mindset — the defenders who protect systems, data, and users from attacks.

Defensive Security involves:
- Understanding what needs to be protected
- Gaining visibility into the environment
- Applying security controls to prevent, detect, and mitigate threats
- Aligning defenses with the **CIA Triad** (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)

## Defender Mindset

Defenders must think like attackers to anticipate threats. Instead of seeing systems as separate parts, they view the infrastructure as an interconnected chain. Attackers often compromise one system and pivot to others.

Key Defender Principles:
- **Threat Anticipation**: Always ask “What if?” and imagine attack paths
- **Attack Awareness**: Understand common attack techniques and stages
- **Risk Prioritization**: Focus on protecting high-value assets first
- **Continuous Adaptation**: Defenses must evolve as threats change

## Core Defender Responsibilities

- **Prevention**: Stop attacks before they happen (firewalls, patching, antivirus)
- **Detection**: Monitor systems and identify suspicious activity (logs, alerts)
- **Mitigation**: Limit damage during an incident (isolate systems, block IPs)
- **Analysis**: Investigate what happened and why
- **Response & Improvement**: Recover and strengthen defenses afterward

## Key Terminology

- **SOC (Security Operations Center)**: Team responsible for continuous monitoring and responding to security alerts
- **Client Infrastructure**: The networks, servers, devices, and applications that belong to an organization
- **Visibility**: The ability to monitor and see activity across systems
- **Threat**: Any potential danger (hacker, malware, etc.) that could harm systems or data
- **Risk**: The likelihood and potential impact of a threat succeeding

## City Analogy Summary

Defenders treat client infrastructure like protecting a city:
- Employee devices → Homes
- Web servers → Shops/Public buildings
- Mail servers → Post office
- Firewalls → City gates
- Internet → Outside the city walls
