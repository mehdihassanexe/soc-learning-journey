## What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is the **most fundamental model** in cybersecurity.  
It stands for **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability** — the three core principles that security professionals try to protect.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Confidentiality
- Protecting information from unauthorized access.
- Only authorized people should be able to see sensitive data.
- Examples: Encryption, strong passwords, access controls.
- Real-world breach: Someone stealing credit card details.

### 2. Integrity
- Ensuring data is accurate and not tampered with.
- Data should not be modified by unauthorized users.
- Examples: Hashing, digital signatures, checksums.
- Real-world breach: Attacker changes salary numbers in a company database.

### 3. Availability
- Making sure systems and data are accessible when needed.
- Systems should not be taken down by attacks or failures.
- Examples: Backups, DDoS protection, redundant servers.
- Real-world breach: Ransomware locking access to important files.

## Memory Hook

**CIA = "Keep it Secret, Keep it Safe, Keep it Available"**

- **C**onfidentiality = Secret  
- **I**ntegrity = Safe (untampered)  
- **A**vailability = Available

## Why It Matters for SOC / Blue Team

- Almost every security incident affects at least one part of the CIA Triad.
- SOC analysts use the CIA Triad to assess risk and prioritize incidents.
- Helps in incident response: "Was confidentiality breached? Was integrity affected?"
